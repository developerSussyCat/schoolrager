I hate TA

Do you realize that if Kellen Heller would have died, there never would have been a book about her? Honestly, The Miracle Worker is overly sentimental, cheesy, and way too dramatic for its own good. It’s like they’re more interested in turning her story into some inspirational legend rather than showing the real, messy, uncomfortable truth. Annie Sullivan comes off as this saint-like hero, which feels completely unrealistic and way over the top. The whole thing is just emotional manipulation, trying to make you feel something rather than actually understand what it’s like to be deaf and blind. Honestly, I can’t stand how fake and exaggerated it all feels—like they’re more interested in tugging at your heartstrings than telling a genuine story. as complained by Sussy_Cat
